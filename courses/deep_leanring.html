This page is made for students of this course. Please feel free to email me any suggestion on how to improve this page for you.
Reception time is every Tuesday from 14:00 to 15:00 in my office. (Updated for: 2019-2020)

Final grade: 30% Homework, 70% final project.

Course Goals: In recent years there has been a major breakthrough in the field of artificial neural networks, especially in deep neural networks (namely, networks with many layers). Deep learning is the term coined for the training of such networks, which have shown superiority over other machine learning algorithms in many applications. Nowadays, deep neural networks provide the best performance in tasks such as, voice recognition, object detection, image classification and content suggestion. These networks gradually take over standard machine learning algorithms and we are at the heart of a new generation, where voice recognition and image recognition are based on them fully. In this course we will explain the mathematics at the core of neural networks models, and provide practical tools for using them. The course focuses mainly on image processing and, consequently, on convolutional neural networks.

  1. Honor Code

    You may consult any papers, books, online references, or publicly available implementations for ideas and code that you may want to incorporate into your strategy or algorithm, so long as you clearly cite your sources in your code and your project description.   2. Collaboration Policy

    You can work in teams of up to 2 people. Note, that 2-people final projects should be bigger and with better results then a single person's final project.

  3. Project Proposal

    The project proposal should be one page, no images, font "airel" 12. Your project proposal should describe:
    1. What is the problem that you will be investigating? Why is it interesting?
    2. What data will you use? If you are collecting new data, how will you do it?
    3. What method or algorithm are you proposing? do you improve exsiting method or algorithm, if so - how?
    4. How will you evaluate your results?
    5. How will you present the results of the algorithm? (e.g. plots or figures)
