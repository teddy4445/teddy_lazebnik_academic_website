Teaching Philosophy

I believe that there are a few essential elements that are conducive to learning.

First, students are adults and therefore no parenthood or dominancy at all. Students can take responsibility for there actions and results. They can handle the subject and manage there time - I just here to share a piece of advice only when I asked too.

Second, students must know why this subject is good. Always start a new subject with motivation from the real world and give examples of why this subject is worthy to be learned.

Third, students should be able to have choices and let their curiosity direct their learning. If the students are interested in some related subject - find time for this subject even if it is not part of the original program.

Forth, honestly, honestly, and honestly again. Honestly from both sides is the only way to get the destination both sides want.

Fifth, give a lot "do it yourself" tasks. A real study was done by solving problems on your own. I am here to guide the process but nothing more. The results should come from the students.

Six, extra credit for creativity and curiosity. no extra credit for trying hard without results.

Seven, if there is a chance to use technology to enhance the learning experience in class - use it.
